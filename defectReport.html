Defect Code Explanation

Electrical Installation and Contestable Service work - MAJOR DEFECTS do not comply with current edition of AS/NZS 3000, Wiring Rules, the Service and Installation Rules of NSW and other relevant standards and is considered to present a high risk safety hazard to life, health or property if Major Defects are detected during an inspection by AUS-EV, the Inspecting Officer may temporarily isolate or disconnect the defective work where possible. If effective isolation cannot be achieved the electricity supply to the entire installation or service connection may be disconnected. It is the obligation of the Installing Contractor, Accredited Service Provider and the Customer to rectify defective work within the period nominated on this form.

The following codes will be used to classify Major Defects:

A. Exposed live parts:

1. Exposed LIVE terminals on equipment that are accessible by unauthorised persons, without the use of a tool or key. This does not include vacant lampholders and fuse bases.

2. Exposed Conductors of unterminated or damaged cables, which can be energised by the operation of a switch, circuit-breaker or insertion of a fuse. This includes cables with open circuits, which cannot be readily located

3. Bare aerial conductors in accessible positions without the use of a ladder.

4. Bush Fire Related Issues, that may cause a risk of igniting a bush fire.

B. Earthing System:

1. Open circuit or high resistance of the protective earthing conductors or exposed conductive parts, that will not allow the overcurrent protective device to operate. This also includes where a fault of negligible impedance could occur and the automatic disconnection of the supply will not occur in the specified time. (Note! The resistance of the main earthing conductor should be no greater than 0.5 Ohms)

2. Unearthed exposed conductive parts of electrical equipment, which is in an earthed situation.

C. Insulation Resistance:

1. Insulation resistance less than 1 Meg Ohm between the circuit conductors and between circuit conductors and earth on new circuits and 250,000 Ohms on other circuits (no appliances connected).

2. Insulation resistance less than 10,000 Ohms between live parts and earthed parts of appliances which incorporates a heating element.

3. Insulation resistance less than 1 Meg Ohm on other low voltage equipment.

NOTE: The minimum safety requirement for insulation resistance between live conductors and earth is 1 Meg Ohm as per AS/NZS 3000 the Wiring Rules (Appliances Connected).

D. Overloaded Equipment:

1. Circuits installed that do not facilitate safe operation. Socket outlets, switches, switchboard equipment, cables and accessories operating in excess of 125% of current rating. The current rating is determined by the maximum demand of the portion of the installation or equipment supplied by the protective device, cable or accessory.

2. Appliances and cables which have overheated to such an extent that serious damage or fire has occurred or could be expected to occur.

E. Devices for Protection

1. No overcurrent or RCD device provided where required.

F. Polarity:

1. Incorrect connection of active, neutral and earthing conductors at socket outlets, lampholders, switchboard equipment and appliances.

2. Isolating device not operating in active conductor(s).

G. Unsuitable Equipment:

1. Equipment exposed to the weather or other damp situation, which is not adequately protected against the direct ingress of water.

2. Equipment installed in a hazardous location, which is not of an explosion protected type or equivalent.

3. Equipment incorrectly selected and installed so that it is likely to cause an adverse effect on the electrical installation.

4. Equipment installed for the supply of fire and smoke control equipment and lifts, which does not provide the required level of protection against fire and mechanical damage.

Notes:
  1. AUS-EV is required to ensure defective electrical installation and contestable work defects are rectified. It is illegal for a person to use any electrical installation or equipment which has been disconnected by AUS-EV due to defects until the defects have been rectified and AUS-EV notified on either a Certificate of Compliance-Electrical Work (CCEW) or Notification of Service Work (NOSW) form.
  2. If the defective work is not rectified within the nominated timeframe, AUS-EV can either disconnect the supply or repair the installation and charge the responsible ASP or Electrical Contractor.
  3. Failure to Test work will be considered as a Major Defect.
